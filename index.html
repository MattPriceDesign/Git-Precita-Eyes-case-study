  3. Founded 40 years ago and run by long time mission residents
  4. Offers tours that explain the Mission’s culture and history
  5. Offers classes where murals can be collaboratively created with community members

Precita Eyess web layout does not communicate its mission, or present their art satisfactory. Their website could be a connection point between potential members and the community but it’s poor disign breaks the connection point and leaves visitors with the burdon of personal research

With this data, we created a problem statement.

The Problem San Francisco transplants feel disconnected from the local community, and worry about their role in its gentrification and cultural dilution.

We conducted user tested to better understand what was or wasn't working with their website

Based on our User testing we made four guidlines that would define a sucessful redesign

  1. Made their mission clear
  2. Showcased their art well
  3. Communicate the credibility they have
  4. Made visitors want to paint The City

Finding the right layout

We remove nested navigation

S-shaped layout breaks information and artwork into bite sized chucks for new visitors to take in and quickly understanding what the page offers.
